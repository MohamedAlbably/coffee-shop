Mocha

Mocha is a popular coffee drink that combines espresso, chocolate, and steamed milk. It's basically a chocolate-flavored caffe latte. The name "mocha" originally comes from the port of Mocha in Yemen, which was famous for exporting coffee with natural chocolatey notes.

Espresso

Espresso is a concentrated form of coffee made by forcing hot water (around 90–96°C) through finely ground, compacted coffee using high pressure (usually 9 bars). It's the base for many popular coffee drinks like latte, cappuccino, and mocha It’s served in small shots and has a strong, rich flavor, topped with a golden foam layer called crema.

Robusta

Robusta is one of the two main species of coffee beans, the other being Arabica Its scientific name is Coffea canephora, and it’s widely grown in Africa, Southeast Asia, and parts of Brazil.
